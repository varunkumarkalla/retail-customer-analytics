ANALYSIS 2: Normality Test
 Business Question:
Does Revenue follow a normal distribution?

Objective: Determine whether Revenue follows a normal distribution.

Method: Shapiro-Wilk Test

Null Hypothesis (H0): Revenue follows a normal distribution.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1): Revenue does not follow a normal distribution.

Results:

Statistic = 0.239956

P-value = 1.8255e-89

Decision: Reject Null Hypothesis

Interpretation:  The P-value is significantly smaller than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, Revenue does not follow a normal distribution.

Business Insight: Customer spending is highly uneven. Most transactions are relatively small, while a small number of transactions contribute exceptionally high revenue.

Impact on Future Analysis: Because Revenue is not normally distributed, non-parametric statistical tests such as the Mann-Whitney U Test should be considered alongside traditional parametric tests.

ANALYSIS 3: VARIANCE TEST
Business Question:
Do UK and Germany customers
have equal variance: Variance Equality Test

Objective: Determine whether revenue variance differs between United Kingdom and Germany customers.

Method: Levene Test

Null Hypothesis (H0): Revenue variance is equal across both countries.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1): Revenue variance differs across countries.

Results:

Statistic = 1.199894

P-value = 0.273343

Decision: Fail to Reject Null Hypothesis

Interpretation: The p-value is greater than 0.05.

Therefore, there is insufficient evidence to conclude that revenue variability differs between the United Kingdom and Germany.

Business Insight:

Customer spending variation appears statistically similar in both countries.

Conclusion:

Equal variance assumption is satisfied.

ANALYSIS 4 : T-TEST

Business Question:
Do UK and Germany customers
spend differently: Independent T-Test

Objective :

Determine whether customer spending differs significantly between customers from the United Kingdom and Germany.

Business Question :

Do customers from the United Kingdom and Germany spend the same amount on average, or is there a statistically significant difference in their purchasing behavior?

Method :

Independent Two-Sample T-Test

Groups Compared

1. United Kingdom Customers

2. Germany Customers

Null Hypothesis (H0) :

Mean customer revenue in the United Kingdom is equal to mean customer revenue in Germany.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1) :

Mean customer revenue in the United Kingdom is not equal to mean customer revenue in Germany.

Results :

Test Statistic = -13.5180

P-value = 1.3819e-41

Decision :

Reject Null Hypothesis (H0)

Reason :

The obtained p-value is significantly smaller than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, there is strong statistical evidence that average customer spending differs between the United Kingdom and Germany.

Average Revenue Comparison :

United Kingdom Mean Revenue = 20.54

Germany Mean Revenue = 25.87

Difference = 5.32

Interpretation :

Germany customers spend significantly more per transaction than customers from the United Kingdom.

The observed difference is statistically significant and is highly unlikely to have occurred due to random chance.

The negative test statistic indicates that the average revenue of the United Kingdom group is lower than the average revenue of the Germany group.

Business Insight :

Customer purchasing behavior varies significantly across countries.

Country appears to be an important factor influencing customer spending patterns and overall revenue generation.

This finding suggests that customer behavior cannot be assumed to be identical across different geographical markets.

Business Recommendation :

Country-specific pricing strategies, promotional campaigns, loyalty programs, and customer engagement initiatives should be considered.

Germany represents a higher-value customer segment based on average transaction revenue and may justify increased marketing investment and customer retention efforts.

Visualization :

Conclusion :

The Independent T-Test confirmed that customer spending behavior differs significantly between the United Kingdom and Germany.

Germany customers generate higher average transaction revenue than customers from the United Kingdom, making country-level segmentation an important consideration for future business decisions and marketing strategies.

ANALYSIS 5 : ANOVA Test

Objective: Determine whether average customer revenue differs across multiple countries.

Business Question: Do customers from the United Kingdom, Germany, and France spend the same amount on average?

Method:

One-Way ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)

Countries Compared

1. United Kingdom

2. Germany

3. France

Null Hypothesis (H0):

Average customer revenue is equal across all countries.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):

At least one country has a different average customer revenue.

Results :

Test Statistic = 7.4158

P-value = 0.0006017

Decision :

Reject Null Hypothesis

Interpretation :

The p-value is significantly smaller than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, there is sufficient statistical evidence to conclude that average customer revenue differs across the countries analyzed.

Business Insight :

Customer purchasing behavior is not identical across geographical markets.

Country-level differences influence spending patterns and revenue generation.

This supports the need for localized marketing, pricing, and customer engagement strategies.

Conclusion :

At least one country's average customer revenue differs significantly from the others.

Country should be considered an important factor in customer analytics and business decision-making.

ANALYSIS 6: Chi-Square Test of Independence

Objective :

Determine whether Country and Month are associated.

Business Question :

Does purchasing behavior vary across countries and months?

Method :

Chi-Square Test of Independence

Variables Analyzed

1. Country

2. Month

Null Hypothesis (H0) :

Country and Month are independent.

There is no association between Country and Month.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1) :

Country and Month are associated.

There is a relationship between Country and Month.

Results :

Chi-Square Statistic = 22755.0491

P-value = 0.0

Degrees of Freedom = 440

Decision :

Reject Null Hypothesis

Interpretation :

The p-value is significantly smaller than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, there is strong statistical evidence that Country and Month are associated.

Business Insight :

Customer purchasing behavior varies across geographical markets and time periods.

Different countries exhibit different purchasing patterns throughout the year.

This indicates that seasonality and customer demand are influenced by geographical location.

Business Recommendation :

Marketing campaigns, inventory planning, and promotional strategies should be customized for different countries and seasons rather than applying a single global strategy.

Conclusion :

Country and Month are not independent.

Geographical location plays an important role in determining monthly purchasing behavior.

ANALYSIS 7: Mann-Whitney U Test

Objective :

Determine whether customer spending differs between the United Kingdom and Germany without assuming normal distribution.

Business Question :

Do customers from the United Kingdom and Germany exhibit different spending behavior?

Method :

Mann-Whitney U Test

Reason for Using This Test :

The Shapiro-Wilk Test indicated that Revenue does not follow a normal distribution.

Therefore, a non-parametric test was required.

Groups Compared

1. United Kingdom Customers

2. Germany Customers

Null Hypothesis (H0) :

The revenue distributions of the United Kingdom and Germany are the same.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1) :

The revenue distributions of the United Kingdom and Germany differ.

Results :

Test Statistic = 3543237526.0

P-value = 0.0

Decision :

Reject Null Hypothesis

Interpretation :

The p-value is significantly smaller than the significance level of 0.05.

Therefore, there is strong statistical evidence that customer spending behavior differs between the United Kingdom and Germany.

Business Insight :

Customers from different countries exhibit different spending patterns.

Country-specific strategies should be considered when designing marketing campaigns and customer engagement programs.

Conclusion :

The Mann-Whitney U Test confirms the findings obtained from the Independent T-Test.

Customer spending behavior differs significantly between the United Kingdom and Germany.
