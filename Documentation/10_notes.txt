ANALYSIS 8 – Customer Segmentation

Objective:

Classify customers into meaningful business segments based on their purchasing behavior using RFM Analysis.

Method:

Customers were segmented using Recency, Frequency, and Monetary scores.

Segmentation Rules:

VIP Customers:

R_Score >= 4

F_Score >= 4

M_Score >= 4

At Risk Customers:

R_Score <= 2

Frequency greater than median frequency

Regular Customers:

All remaining customers

Results:

VIP Customers = 60

At Risk Customers = 2,519

Regular Customers = 3,299

Total Customers = 5,878

Interpretation:

The majority of customers belong to the Regular segment.

A significant number of customers are classified as At Risk, indicating potential customer churn.

Only a small percentage of customers qualify as VIP customers.

VIP Percentage:

(60 / 5,878) × 100

= 1.02%

Business Insight:

VIP customers represent only 1.02% of the customer base but are likely responsible for a significant portion of business revenue.

At Risk customers represent customers who were previously active but may stop purchasing if retention efforts are not implemented.

Regular customers provide opportunities for future growth and conversion into higher-value segments.

Business Recommendation:

Implement loyalty programs for VIP customers.

Launch retention campaigns for At Risk customers.

Use targeted promotions to encourage Regular customers to increase purchase frequency and spending.

Conclusion:

RFM Analysis successfully segmented customers into actionable business groups and provided valuable insights for customer retention, marketing strategy, and revenue growth initiatives.
