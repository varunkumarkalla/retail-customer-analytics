ANALYSIS 9 – CLV Business Insights

Objective:

Analyze the distribution and business implications of Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).

Summary Statistics:

Total Customers = 5,878

Average CLV = 8,867.71

Median CLV = 2,603.22

Minimum CLV = 8.85

Maximum CLV = 1,742,961.12

Standard Deviation = 43,322.56

25th Percentile = 1,026.84

75th Percentile = 6,744.92

Interpretation:

The average CLV is significantly higher than the median CLV.

This indicates that a small number of customers generate extremely high lifetime value while the majority generate relatively modest value.

The maximum CLV is substantially larger than the average customer CLV.

Business Insight:

Customer value is highly concentrated.

A small percentage of customers contribute a large portion of future revenue.

This follows the Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule), where a minority of customers generate the majority of business value.

Business Recommendation:

Identify and retain high-CLV customers through loyalty programs, premium services, and personalized marketing campaigns.

Conclusion:

Customer Lifetime Value analysis reveals substantial variation in customer value and highlights the importance of customer retention.
